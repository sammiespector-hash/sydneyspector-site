Until this summer, Sydney Spector spent her days inside Princeton University's admissions office, reading real applications and helping decide who got in after graduating from Princeton herself. She knows, first-hand, what makes an admissions reader lean in and what makes them quietly move on to the next folder, because she was the reader.

Now she's leaving admissions to begin law school at Georgetown University. Before she does, she's opening her calendar to a small number of families, translating everything she learned on the inside into a clear, honest plan for your student while it's all still fresh.

Sydney brings both sides to the table. A Princeton graduate who came up through the competitive private-school world, Sydney has felt the pressure your student feels now, and she has seen exactly how the decision gets made on the other end. With extensive experience working with students, Sydney is ready to guide your student through the college application process.

Why families work with a counselor

A great student can still get lost in the pile.

Every year, more accomplished applicants compete for the same handful of seats. Ability isn't usually what decides it. Preparation and positioning are. That's where a guide earns their place.

01

The bar keeps rising

Selective schools now read tens of thousands of excellent files for very few spots. Top grades and scores are the starting line, not the finish.

02

Personality is everything

A reader spends only minutes with each application. What sets admitted students apart is a clear, honest narrative, not a longer list of activities.

03

Confidence is rewarded

School lists, timelines, essays, recommendation planning. Families who start planning early and make deliberate choices hold a real, compounding advantage.

How we work together

A plan that builds confidence, from first call to submission

01

Discovery call

We start with the student, not a template. Goals, strengths, worries, and where they are in the timeline, so any plan fits the real person.

02

Academic preparation

Success in the classroom is the base of your student's application. We will work together in classroom subject tutoring, advanced course selection, and standardized test prep to strengthen your student's academic portfolio.

03

Strategy and school list

A balanced list that's ambitious and realistic, paired with a term-by-term plan for coursework, testing, and activities.

04

Essays and the application

We draw out the student's real story and shape essays that sound like them, not like a consultant. Every part of the application creates a cohesive image of them as a person, not just a student.

05

Final review

Before anything is submitted, the whole application gets a reader's-eye pass, the same read it will get on the other side of the desk.
